Competitive Landscape
Goal: Win any competitive conversation without attacking competitors.
The Rule
Never attack. Raise a question. Find the gap. Attacking competitors makes you look insecure. Asking a question makes you look confident and consultative.
vs. OpenAI
Head-to-Head Differentiators
Win rate: 70% of head-to-head enterprise deals go to Anthropic.
Safety: Constitutional AI + PBC vs. OpenAI's restructured-away-from-nonprofit story.
MCP: Open ecosystem vs. closed. More connectors, more stickiness.
Computer Use: Live. OpenAI hasn't shipped this at scale.
Market share: 73% of new AI buyers choosing Anthropic (Ramp data, early 2026). OpenAI at 27%.
vs. Google Gemini
Key Angles
Cloud independence: Deploy on AWS Bedrock, Azure, or direct API. Most enterprises aren't Google-only.
Independent governance: Enterprise legal teams don't want a hyperscaler owning their AI vendor.
MCP open ecosystem vs. Google's walled garden.
vs. Microsoft Copilot
Key Angles
Copilot is a wrapper on OpenAI — not an independent model roadmap.
Claude Code vs. GitHub Copilot: agentic (does the task) vs. assistive (completes lines). Different capability tier.
MCP connects to any system — Copilot is deeply Microsoft-native.
Objection Handling Framework
1
Acknowledge: "Makes sense — a lot of our best customers started there too."
2
Explore: "Can I ask how you're using it currently? What's the main workflow?"
3
Find the gap: "What's one thing you wish it did better or handled differently?"
4
Position Claude in the gap: Connect the specific gap to a specific Claude capability.
5
Small CTA: "Worth 15 minutes to see if we fill that specific gap?"

Cold Email Formula
Under 125 Words — 49% Higher Reply Rate
Line 1:The signal. Proves you looked. Specific and recent.
Line 2:A question that raises a specific doubt — not a pitch. Make them think.
Line 3:One CTA. 15-minute call. That's it.
Subject:Specific. No spam words. Reference the signal if possible.
Cold Call Structure
4-Part Structure
Opener:"Do you have 27 seconds?" — increases connect-to-conversation by 40%.
Hook:Reference the signal. Why them, why now.
Question:Don't pitch — make them curious. Raise a doubt.
CTA:15 minutes. That's the only ask.
Great BDR Day Structure

Morning (90-min block): Cold calls. Highest energy. No email, no Slack.

Midday: Account research and build. Map the org, find the signals, prep the outreach.

Afternoon: CRM hygiene, follow-ups, replies. Protect the afternoon for reactive work.

End of day: 10-minute self-debrief on metrics. What worked, what didn't, what changes tomorrow.
