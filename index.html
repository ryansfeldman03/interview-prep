Interview Strategy — How to Actually Run the Call
Opening line: "Thanks for making time, Carl — I've been looking forward to this. I'll be direct and we can go as deep as you want anywhere." Sets the tone: not nervous, respect his time, ready to go.
When you blank: "Let me think about that for a second — I want to give you a specific answer rather than a general one." Three-second pause sounds confident. Rambling sounds nervous. Always pick the pause.
Mirror his language: If Carl says "pipeline build" — use it back. If he says "prospecting motion" — use it. Mirroring signals you're listening, not waiting to perform your next prepared answer.
Bridge between topics: Let Carl lead the flow. When topics shift, bridge naturally: "That actually connects to something I did at FiberSeal..." Don't force every transition into a prepared story.
Closing line: "Before we close — I want to be direct. I'm not exploring this casually. I've been running on Claude for months and I've thought hard about why Anthropic specifically. MCP — Model Context Protocol
Open standard · Platform moat · Turns Claude from a tool into infrastructure
▾
Plain English
MCP is an open protocol Anthropic built that lets Claude connect to any external tool or data source — Slack, Salesforce, Gmail, databases, calendars, GitHub, anything. Without MCP, every integration requires custom development. With MCP, you build a connector once and it works everywhere. Anthropic made it open source, so anyone can build connectors, and the ecosystem grows on its own.
Analogy: USB-C. Before USB-C you had 5 different cables for 5 different devices. MCP is the USB-C of AI integrations — one standard that connects everything. OpenAI keeps their ecosystem closed. Anthropic opened theirs, which means the platform gets stickier with every new connector that gets built.
Why Enterprises Buy
Enterprise stickiness. Once Claude is integrated into your CRM, Slack, and internal databases through MCP, it's not a tool you log into — it's infrastructure you run on. Removing it means removing all those integrations. That's a completely different kind of switching cost than "we prefer a different chatbot." More integrations = harder to rip out = the platform moat compounds over time.
Your BDR opener: "What's your team's current stack for CRM and internal communication? Because the reason MCP matters for enterprise is it means Claude lives natively inside all of it — your reps don't have to context-switch to another tool."

Constitutional AI
Safety baked in, not bolted on · Core training methodology · Enterprise compliance story
▾
Plain English
Constitutional AI is how Anthropic trains Claude's values, not as an afterthought. The model is trained to evaluate its own outputs against a set of principles — it critiques itself and revises until it meets the standard. This is different from content filters that try to block bad outputs after the model produces them. The safety is part of the model's reasoning, not a fence around it.
Analogy: Most AI safety is like a bouncer checking IDs at the door — it blocks certain outputs on the way out. Constitutional AI is like good judgment trained in from childhood — the model has internalized the principles, not just learned which outputs get flagged. The bouncer can be tricked or bypassed. Internalized judgment is far more robust.
Why Enterprises Buy
Regulated industries — healthcare, financial services, legal — can't just accept "we have guardrails" from a vendor. They need a structural answer to "how do you guarantee outputs meet our compliance requirements?" Constitutional AI is that structural answer. Combined with the PBC (Public Benefit Corporation) legal structure, Anthropic is the only AI vendor that can say safety is both architecturally baked in AND legally required. That's a fundamentally different conversation in an enterprise procurement meeting.
Your BDR opener: "When your legal or compliance team gets involved in evaluating AI vendors, the question that usually blocks deals is 'how do you guarantee safe outputs?' Most vendors say 'we have content filters.' We have Constitutional AI — safety trained in systematically — and a PBC legal structure that makes safety research mandatory. That's a different answer."

The RSP — Responsible Scaling Policy
Constitutional AI is the methodology. The RSP is the commitment. Anthropic's Responsible Scaling Policy defines AI Safety Levels (ASL-1 through ASL-4+) and commits to not training or deploying models past each threshold without having specific safeguards in place first. Claude Opus 4.6 triggered ASL-3 — Anthropic activated enhanced security controls and access restrictions before releasing it. The commitment: if a model hits the next threshold and the safety measures aren't ready, they pause. They don't ship. This is verifiable and public — not a promise.
→ Sales line: "OpenAI restructured away from its safety mission. Anthropic publishes what they won't do and legally holds themselves to it. For a CISO evaluating a 5-year contract, that's a different conversation."
